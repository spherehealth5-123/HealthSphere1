Yellow Fever

Overview

Yellow Fever is a viral infection spread by mosquitoes, characterized by fever, jaundice, and bleeding tendencies.

Causes

  • Infection by the Yellow Fever virus.
  • Transmission through Aedes and Haemagogus mosquitoes.

Symptoms

  • High fever and chills.
  • Headache, backache, and muscle pain.
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes).
  • Bleeding and nausea in severe cases.

Treatment

  • No specific antiviral treatment.
  • Supportive care: hydration, fever control, and monitoring for organ damage.

Prevention

  • Vaccination against Yellow Fever.
  • Mosquito control and using repellents.
  • Protective clothing to prevent mosquito bites.
