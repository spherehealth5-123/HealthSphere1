Restless Leg Syndrome

Overview

Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS) is a neurological disorder causing an irresistible urge to move the legs, usually in the evening or at night.

Causes

  • Genetic factors
  • Iron deficiency
  • Chronic conditions like kidney failure

Symptoms

  • Tingling or crawling sensations
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Temporary relief with movement

Treatment

Iron supplements, dopamine-related medications, and lifestyle changes (exercise, sleep hygiene).

Prevention

Maintain balanced nutrition, avoid caffeine, and treat underlying health conditions.
