Vitamin Deficiencies

Overview

Vitamin deficiencies occur when the body does not get enough essential vitamins, leading to impaired body functions and diseases.

Causes

  • Poor diet lacking essential nutrients.
  • Digestive disorders reducing absorption.
  • Chronic illnesses or medications.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue and weakness.
  • Bone pain or fractures (Vitamin D deficiency).
  • Bleeding gums (Vitamin C deficiency).
  • Anemia (Vitamin B12 or Folate deficiency).

Treatment

  • Balanced diet with fresh fruits, vegetables, and proteins.
  • Vitamin supplements as prescribed.
  • Treating underlying medical conditions.

Prevention

  • Eat a variety of nutrient-rich foods.
  • Maintain adequate sun exposure for Vitamin D.
  • Regular health checkups to detect deficiencies early.
