Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

Overview

A urinary tract infection (UTI) is a bacterial infection affecting any part of the urinary system—kidneys, bladder, ureters, or urethra.

Causes

  • Bacteria (commonly E. coli)
  • Poor hygiene
  • Catheter use
  • Weakened immune system

Symptoms

  • Burning during urination
  • Frequent urge to urinate
  • Cloudy or strong-smelling urine
  • Pelvic pain

Treatment

Antibiotics, hydration, and pain relief medications.

Prevention

Stay hydrated, maintain hygiene, and urinate after intercourse.
