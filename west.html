West Nile Virus

Overview

West Nile Virus is a mosquito-borne illness that can cause mild flu-like symptoms or severe neurological disease.

Causes

  • Bite of infected mosquitoes.
  • Rarely through blood transfusion or organ transplant.

Symptoms

  • Fever, headache, fatigue.
  • Skin rash, swollen lymph nodes.
  • Severe cases: meningitis, encephalitis, paralysis.

Treatment

  • No specific treatment; supportive care.
  • Pain relievers and fluids for mild cases.
  • Hospitalization for neurological complications.

Prevention

  • Use insect repellent.
  • Eliminate standing water near homes.
  • Wear protective clothing outdoors.
