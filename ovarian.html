Ovarian Cancer

Overview

Ovarian Cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the ovaries and often goes undetected until it spreads within the pelvis or abdomen.

Causes

Exact causes are unknown but genetic mutations (BRCA1, BRCA2), family history, and hormonal factors increase risk.

Symptoms

  • Abdominal bloating or swelling
  • Pelvic pain
  • Frequent urination
  • Loss of appetite
  • Unexplained weight loss

Treatment

Treatment involves surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and sometimes radiation.

Prevention

Risk can be lowered with genetic screening, birth control pills, and prophylactic surgery for high-risk women.
