Quinidine-Induced Thrombocytopenia

Overview

Quinidine-Induced Thrombocytopenia is a rare drug reaction where quinidine causes a severe drop in platelet count.

Causes

  • Adverse immune reaction to quinidine medication.
  • Destruction of platelets triggered by antibodies.

Symptoms

  • Unusual bruising and bleeding.
  • Small red or purple spots on skin (petechiae).
  • Nosebleeds and gum bleeding.

Treatment

Immediate discontinuation of quinidine, supportive care, and sometimes corticosteroids or platelet transfusion.

Prevention

  • Avoid quinidine if allergic or previously affected.
  • Regular monitoring of platelet counts during treatment.
