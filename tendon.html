Quadriceps Tendon Rupture

Overview

Quadriceps Tendon Rupture occurs when the tendon attaching the quadriceps muscle to the kneecap tears, often from trauma or overuse.

Causes

  • Direct injury to the knee.
  • Sudden heavy load on the quadriceps.
  • Chronic diseases like diabetes or kidney failure weakening tendons.

Symptoms

  • Sudden pain above the kneecap.
  • Inability to straighten the leg.
  • Swelling and bruising around the knee.

Treatment

Surgical repair is usually required, followed by physiotherapy to restore mobility and strength.

Prevention

  • Avoid overloading the knees.
  • Warm up properly before exercise.
  • Treat chronic illnesses that affect tendons.
