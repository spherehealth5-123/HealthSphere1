E. Coli Infection

Overview

E. coli infection is caused by certain strains of the bacteria Escherichia coli, which can lead to foodborne illness.

Causes

Contaminated food or water, undercooked beef, and poor hygiene practices.

Symptoms

  • Severe stomach cramps
  • Diarrhea (sometimes bloody)
  • Vomiting
  • Fever

Treatment

Most cases resolve with rest and fluids; severe infections may require hospitalization.

Prevention

Cook meat thoroughly, wash hands, and avoid contaminated water or food.
