Quiet Eye Syndrome

Overview

Quiet Eye Syndrome is a rare visual disorder where the ability to fixate and track objects is impaired, affecting vision and coordination.

Causes

  • Neurological conditions affecting eye movement control.
  • Ocular muscle dysfunction.
  • Brain injury or developmental issues.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty focusing on objects.
  • Reduced coordination during visual tasks.
  • Eye strain and headaches.

Treatment

Treatment includes vision therapy, eye exercises, and in some cases, medications to support neurological function.

Prevention

  • Early screening for visual problems in children.
  • Protect eyes and head from injury.
