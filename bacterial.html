Bacterial Meningitis

Overview

Bacterial meningitis is a serious infection of the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord (meninges). It is considered a medical emergency because it can progress quickly and be life-threatening.

Causes

The most common bacteria that cause meningitis include Neisseria meningitidis, Streptococcus pneumoniae, and Haemophilus influenzae. The infection often spreads through respiratory droplets or close contact.

Symptoms

  • Severe headache
  • Stiff neck
  • Sudden high fever
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Confusion or difficulty concentrating
  • Seizures
  • Sensitivity to light

Treatment

Immediate treatment with intravenous antibiotics and corticosteroids is essential. Hospitalization is required, and supportive care may include fluids, oxygen, and monitoring of vital functions.

Prevention

Vaccination against meningococcal, pneumococcal, and Haemophilus influenzae type B (Hib) significantly reduces the risk of bacterial meningitis. Good hygiene and avoiding close contact with infected individuals also help prevent infection.
