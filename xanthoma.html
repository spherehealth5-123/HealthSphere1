Xanthoma

Overview

Xanthomas are fatty deposits that form under the skin, often associated with high lipid levels.

Causes

  • High cholesterol or triglyceride levels.
  • Diabetes or liver disease.

Symptoms

  • Yellowish bumps or plaques on skin.
  • May appear on elbows, knees, or tendons.

Treatment

  • Manage underlying lipid disorders.
  • Laser therapy or surgery for cosmetic removal.

Prevention

  • Maintain healthy diet and cholesterol levels.
  • Regular monitoring of blood lipids.
