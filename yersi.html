Yersiniosis

Overview

An infection caused by Yersinia bacteria, commonly affecting the intestines and causing gastrointestinal symptoms.

Causes

  • Contaminated food, especially pork products.
  • Drinking contaminated water.
  • Contact with infected animals.

Symptoms

  • Diarrhea, often bloody.
  • Abdominal pain, fever, and nausea.
  • Joint pain in some cases.

Treatment

  • Usually self-limiting; hydration is important.
  • Antibiotics in severe cases.

Prevention

  • Cook meat thoroughly.
  • Practice good food hygiene and handwashing.
