Zoster (Shingles)

Overview

Zoster is caused by reactivation of the varicella-zoster virus, causing painful rash along nerves and postherpetic neuralgia.

Causes

  • Reactivation of dormant varicella-zoster virus
  • Weak immune system or aging

Symptoms

  • Pain, burning, tingling on one side of the body
  • Red rash with fluid-filled blisters
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Headache

Treatment

  • Antiviral medications (acyclovir, valacyclovir)
  • Pain relievers (acetaminophen, NSAIDs, or prescription pain meds)
  • Corticosteroids in some cases

Prevention

  • Shingles vaccine for adults 50+
  • Avoid contact with unvaccinated individuals
