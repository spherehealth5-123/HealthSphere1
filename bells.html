Bell's Palsy

Overview

Bell's palsy is sudden weakness or paralysis of facial muscles, usually temporary.

Causes

Linked to viral infections such as herpes simplex virus.

Symptoms

  • Facial drooping
  • Loss of taste
  • Tearing and drooling
  • Inability to close one eye

Treatment

Corticosteroids, antiviral drugs, physical therapy.

Prevention

No certain prevention, but managing viral infections may reduce risk.
