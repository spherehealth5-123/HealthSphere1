Xanthoma Disseminatum

Overview

Xanthoma Disseminatum is a rare disorder characterized by the growth of yellowish skin lesions due to abnormal lipid deposits.

Causes

  • Abnormal lipid metabolism.
  • Exact genetic or environmental triggers are unclear.

Symptoms

  • Multiple yellowish-brown papules on skin and mucous membranes.
  • Lesions may be widespread and persistent.
  • Occasionally associated with diabetes insipidus.

Treatment

  • Lipid-lowering therapy (statins, fibrates).
  • Corticosteroids for inflammation.
  • Laser or surgical removal of lesions in severe cases.

Prevention

  • Manage underlying lipid disorders.
  • Regular medical monitoring for early intervention.
