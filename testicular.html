Testicular Cancer

Overview

Testicular cancer develops in the testicles and is most common in younger men between ages 15–35.

Causes

  • Undescended testicles
  • Genetic predisposition
  • HIV infection

Symptoms

  • Lump/swelling in testicle
  • Pain or discomfort
  • Heaviness in scrotum

Treatment

Orchiectomy (surgical removal), radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and surveillance.

Prevention

Regular self-examinations and early detection.
