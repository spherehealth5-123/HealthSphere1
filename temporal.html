Temporal Lobe Epilepsy

Overview

Temporal lobe epilepsy (TLE) is a chronic neurological condition where recurrent seizures originate in the temporal lobe of the brain.

Causes

  • Head injury
  • Brain infections (meningitis, encephalitis)
  • Genetic predisposition

Symptoms

  • Seizures with impaired awareness
  • Strange sensations (déjà vu, fear)
  • Uncontrolled movements (lip smacking, hand gestures)

Treatment

Anti-seizure medications, surgery, and vagus nerve stimulation.

Prevention

Preventing head trauma and treating brain infections promptly.
