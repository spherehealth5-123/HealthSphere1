Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

Overview

Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (EDS) is a group of genetic disorders affecting connective tissue, leading to overly flexible joints and fragile skin.

Causes

Mutations in genes responsible for collagen production and structure.

Symptoms

  • Hypermobile joints
  • Fragile, stretchy skin
  • Chronic pain
  • Easy bruising

Treatment

No cure; treatment includes physical therapy, pain management, and surgery for complications.

Prevention

Genetic counseling and avoiding injury through careful lifestyle adjustments.
