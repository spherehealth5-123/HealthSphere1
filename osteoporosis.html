Osteoporosis

Overview

Osteoporosis is a bone disease that occurs when the body loses too much bone or makes too little, leading to fragile bones.

Causes

Caused by aging, hormonal changes, low calcium/vitamin D, sedentary lifestyle, and genetics.

Symptoms

  • Frequent bone fractures
  • Loss of height
  • Stooped posture
  • Back pain

Treatment

Treatment includes bisphosphonates, hormone therapy, supplements, and exercise.

Prevention

Prevented by adequate calcium/vitamin D, regular exercise, and avoiding smoking/alcohol.
