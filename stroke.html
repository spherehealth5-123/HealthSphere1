Stroke

Overview

A Stroke occurs when blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients.

Causes

  • Blood clot (ischemic stroke)
  • Ruptured blood vessel (hemorrhagic stroke)
  • Transient ischemic attack (mini-stroke)

Symptoms

  • Sudden numbness or weakness
  • Confusion, trouble speaking
  • Loss of balance or coordination

Treatment

Immediate medical intervention, clot-busting drugs, surgery, and rehabilitation therapies.

Prevention

Managing blood pressure, healthy diet, regular exercise, controlling diabetes and cholesterol.
