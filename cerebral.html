Cerebral Palsy

Overview

Cerebral palsy (CP) is a group of neurological disorders affecting movement, posture, and muscle coordination.

Causes

Brain injury during pregnancy, birth complications, infections, or lack of oxygen to the brain.

Symptoms

  • Muscle stiffness or weakness
  • Difficulty walking
  • Speech and coordination problems
  • Seizures in some cases

Treatment

No cure. Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, medications, and surgery help manage symptoms.

Prevention

Proper prenatal care, safe childbirth practices, and early treatment of infections.
