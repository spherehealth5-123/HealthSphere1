Quadriparesis

Overview

Quadriparesis is weakness in all four limbs, less severe than quadriplegia, and can result from neurological or muscular conditions.

Causes

  • Spinal cord injury.
  • Stroke or brain damage.
  • Neuromuscular disorders like ALS.

Symptoms

  • Weakness in arms and legs.
  • Difficulty walking or holding objects.
  • Fatigue and muscle stiffness.

Treatment

Includes physiotherapy, medications for muscle strength, and in some cases, surgery depending on the cause.

Prevention

  • Prevent head and spinal injuries.
  • Manage chronic diseases effectively.
