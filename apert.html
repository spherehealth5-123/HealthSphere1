Apert Syndrome

Overview

Apert syndrome is a rare genetic disorder characterized by the premature fusion of certain skull bones, affecting the shape of the head and face. It can also cause fusion of fingers and toes (syndactyly).

Causes

Apert syndrome is caused by mutations in the FGFR2 gene, which plays an important role in bone development. This mutation occurs spontaneously and is not usually inherited from parents.

Symptoms

  • Abnormally shaped head and face
  • Bulging, wide-set eyes
  • Fused or webbed fingers and toes
  • Hearing loss
  • Possible developmental delays

Treatment

There is no cure, but treatment focuses on surgery to correct skull and facial abnormalities, separate fused fingers or toes, and therapies to support breathing, hearing, and development.

Prevention

As Apert syndrome is a genetic condition, prevention is not possible. However, genetic counseling can help families understand risks in future pregnancies.
