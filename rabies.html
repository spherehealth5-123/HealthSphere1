Rabies

Overview

Rabies is a viral disease transmitted through bites or scratches from infected animals. It affects the central nervous system and is almost always fatal once symptoms appear.

Causes

  • Bite from rabid animals (dogs, bats, foxes, raccoons)
  • Exposure to saliva of infected animals

Symptoms

  • Fever, headache, and weakness
  • Agitation, confusion, hydrophobia
  • Seizures, paralysis, coma

Treatment

No cure after symptoms appear. Immediate wound cleaning and post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) vaccines are critical.

Prevention

  • Vaccinate pets
  • Avoid contact with stray animals
  • Timely rabies vaccination after exposure
