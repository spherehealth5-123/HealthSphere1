Exotropia

Overview

Exotropia is a type of strabismus where one or both eyes turn outward instead of aligning properly.

Causes

Weak eye muscles, nerve issues, genetics, or medical conditions affecting vision.

Symptoms

  • Outward eye drift
  • Double vision
  • Eye strain
  • Difficulty focusing

Treatment

Glasses, vision therapy, and surgery to realign the eyes.

Prevention

Early vision screenings and regular eye check-ups for children.
