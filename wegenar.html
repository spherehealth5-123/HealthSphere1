Wegener's Granulomatosis

Overview

Wegener's Granulomatosis, now called Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA), is an autoimmune disease causing inflammation of blood vessels in the nose, lungs, and kidneys.

Causes

  • Autoimmune response leading to vasculitis.
  • Exact triggers unknown, possible genetic predisposition.

Symptoms

  • Sinus infections, nosebleeds, or chronic nasal congestion.
  • Shortness of breath and cough.
  • Kidney problems and blood in urine.
  • Fatigue and fever.

Treatment

  • Immunosuppressive therapy (corticosteroids, cyclophosphamide).
  • Rituximab for severe cases.
  • Regular monitoring of kidney and lung function.

Prevention

  • No known prevention; early diagnosis and treatment are key to reducing complications.
