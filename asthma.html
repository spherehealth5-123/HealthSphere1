Asthma

Overview

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition that causes inflammation and narrowing of the airways, leading to difficulty in breathing. It can affect both children and adults, and its severity ranges from mild to life-threatening.

Causes

Asthma is often triggered by allergens, respiratory infections, exercise, cold air, stress, or environmental pollutants. A family history of asthma or allergies increases the risk.

Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest tightness or pain
  • Wheezing when exhaling
  • Frequent coughing, especially at night
  • Difficulty sleeping due to breathing problems

Treatment

Asthma cannot be cured, but it can be managed with inhalers, bronchodilators, corticosteroids, and lifestyle modifications. Long-term management aims to control symptoms and prevent asthma attacks.

Prevention

Avoiding known triggers, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, taking prescribed medications, and regular medical checkups can help reduce the risk of asthma attacks.
