Quinsy (Peritonsillar Abscess)

Overview

Quinsy is a serious complication of tonsillitis, where pus collects near the tonsils causing severe throat pain and difficulty swallowing.

Causes

  • Bacterial infection after untreated tonsillitis.
  • Streptococcus pyogenes is the most common bacteria involved.

Symptoms

  • Severe sore throat (usually one side).
  • Swelling and difficulty swallowing.
  • Fever and ear pain.

Treatment

Treatment includes antibiotics, draining the abscess, and in some cases tonsillectomy.

Prevention

  • Treat tonsillitis promptly.
  • Maintain good oral hygiene.
