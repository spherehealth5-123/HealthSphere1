Waldenstrom's Macroglobulinemia

Overview

Waldenstrom's Macroglobulinemia is a rare type of non-Hodgkin lymphoma causing abnormal white blood cells that produce excess antibodies.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations in B-cells.
  • Exact cause unknown; more common in older adults.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue, weakness, anemia.
  • Easy bruising or bleeding.
  • Enlarged lymph nodes or spleen.

Treatment

  • Chemotherapy and targeted therapies.
  • Plasmapheresis to reduce antibody levels.
  • Supportive care for anemia and infections.

Prevention

  • No guaranteed prevention; routine checkups can help early detection in high-risk adults.
