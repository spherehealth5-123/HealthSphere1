Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN)

Overview

Toxic epidermal necrolysis is a rare, life-threatening skin disorder often caused by a severe drug reaction.

Causes

  • Allergic reaction to medications (sulfa drugs, anticonvulsants, antibiotics)
  • Infections in rare cases

Symptoms

  • Widespread skin peeling
  • Severe blistering
  • Fever and flu-like symptoms

Treatment

Immediate withdrawal of causative drug, intensive care, fluid replacement, and immunosuppressive therapy.

Prevention

Avoid known drug allergies and report adverse drug reactions promptly.
