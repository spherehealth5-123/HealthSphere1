Tuberculosis (TB)

Overview

Tuberculosis is an infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis, mainly affecting the lungs but can spread to other organs.

Causes

  • Bacteria spread through airborne droplets
  • Close contact with infected individuals

Symptoms

  • Chronic cough with blood
  • Night sweats and fever
  • Unexplained weight loss

Treatment

Long-term antibiotic therapy (isoniazid, rifampin, ethambutol, pyrazinamide).

Prevention

BCG vaccine, early detection, avoiding close contact with active TB patients.
