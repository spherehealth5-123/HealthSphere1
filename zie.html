Zieve's Syndrome

Overview

Zieve’s Syndrome is a rare condition associated with alcoholic liver disease, characterized by hemolytic anemia, jaundice, and hyperlipidemia.

Causes

  • Excessive alcohol consumption causing liver damage
  • Breakdown of red blood cells due to liver dysfunction

Symptoms

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
  • Abdominal discomfort
  • Dark urine

Treatment

  • Complete alcohol abstinence
  • Supportive care for anemia and liver function
  • Lipid-lowering treatments if required

Prevention

  • Avoid excessive alcohol consumption
  • Regular monitoring of liver health for at-risk individuals
