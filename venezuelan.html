Venezuelan Hemorrhagic Fever

Overview

A severe viral disease caused by the Guanarito virus, found mainly in Venezuela. It is transmitted from rodents to humans.

Causes

  • Caused by Guanarito arenavirus.
  • Transmitted through rodent excreta or direct contact.

Symptoms

  • High fever
  • Bleeding gums and nose
  • Low blood pressure
  • Organ failure in severe cases

Treatment

  • No specific cure available.
  • Supportive care and blood transfusions.
  • Ribavirin may help if given early.

Prevention

  • Control rodent populations.
  • Avoid exposure in endemic areas.
