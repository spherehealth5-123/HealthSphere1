Q Fever

Overview

Q Fever is a bacterial infection caused by *Coxiella burnetii*. It mainly affects humans who come into contact with infected animals.

Causes

  • Inhalation of dust contaminated by animal waste.
  • Direct contact with infected animals.
  • Consumption of unpasteurized dairy products.

Symptoms

  • High fever and chills.
  • Severe headache and muscle pain.
  • Fatigue and cough.

Treatment

Antibiotics such as doxycycline are effective for treating Q Fever.

Prevention

  • Avoid unpasteurized milk and dairy products.
  • Use protective equipment when handling animals.
  • Maintain good farm hygiene.
