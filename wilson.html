Wilson Disease

Overview

Wilson Disease is a rare genetic disorder that causes copper buildup in the liver, brain, and other organs.

Causes

  • Genetic mutation affecting copper metabolism.
  • Inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue, jaundice, abdominal swelling.
  • Neurological problems like tremors, speech issues.
  • Psychiatric symptoms including mood swings.

Treatment

  • Copper-chelating medications (penicillamine).
  • Zinc therapy to block copper absorption.
  • Liver transplant in severe cases.

Prevention

  • Early genetic testing in families with history.
  • Regular monitoring of liver function.
  • Low-copper diet (avoid shellfish, nuts, chocolate).
