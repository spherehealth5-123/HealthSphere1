Uveal Melanoma

Overview

Uveal melanoma is the most common eye cancer in adults, developing in the uvea (middle layer of the eye).

Causes

  • Genetic mutations
  • Light eye color
  • UV radiation exposure

Symptoms

  • Blurred vision
  • Dark spots on iris
  • Flashes or floaters

Treatment

Radiation therapy, laser treatment, surgery, or enucleation in severe cases.

Prevention

Wearing UV-protective sunglasses and regular eye exams.
