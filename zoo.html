Zoonosis

Overview

Zoonosis refers to diseases transmitted from animals to humans, either through direct contact, bites, or vectors like mosquitoes and ticks.

Causes

  • Bacterial, viral, fungal, or parasitic infections from animals
  • Contaminated food or water
  • Insect or tick bites

Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Rash
  • Diarrhea
  • Muscle pain
  • Respiratory issues

Treatment

  • Antibiotics or antivirals depending on pathogen
  • Supportive care: fluids, rest, and symptom management
  • Hospitalization in severe cases

Prevention

  • Vaccination for pets and livestock
  • Good hygiene when handling animals
  • Proper cooking of meat and pasteurization of milk
  • Vector control (mosquito nets, repellents)
