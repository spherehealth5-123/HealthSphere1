Brucellosis

Overview

Brucellosis is an infectious disease caused by the Brucella bacteria. It spreads from animals to humans, most often through unpasteurized dairy products, direct contact with infected animals, or inhalation of contaminated air.

Causes

Brucellosis is caused by different species of Brucella, such as B. melitensis, B. abortus, B. suis, and B. canis. People at higher risk include farmers, veterinarians, and laboratory workers.

Symptoms

  • Fever (often undulant)
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Joint and muscle pain
  • Excessive sweating
  • Loss of appetite and weight loss

Treatment

Brucellosis is treated with a combination of antibiotics such as doxycycline and rifampicin, usually taken for 6 weeks or more. Early treatment helps prevent chronic infection and complications.

Prevention

Preventive measures include avoiding unpasteurized milk and dairy products, using protective gear when handling animals, and controlling infection in livestock.
