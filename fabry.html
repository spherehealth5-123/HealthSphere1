Fabry Disease

Overview

Fabry disease is a rare genetic disorder that results in the buildup of fat molecules in the body due to enzyme deficiency.

Causes

Mutations in the GLA gene causing deficiency of the alpha-galactosidase A enzyme.

Symptoms

  • Pain in hands and feet
  • Skin rash (angiokeratomas)
  • Kidney and heart problems
  • Hearing loss

Treatment

Enzyme replacement therapy, pain management, and supportive care.

Prevention

Genetic counseling for families at risk.
