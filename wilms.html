Wilms Tumor

Overview

Wilms Tumor is a rare kidney cancer primarily affecting children, causing abdominal swelling and pain.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations affecting kidney development.
  • Associated with syndromes like WAGR, Beckwith-Wiedemann.

Symptoms

  • Abdominal swelling or mass.
  • Fever, blood in urine.
  • Fatigue and loss of appetite.

Treatment

  • Surgical removal of the affected kidney (nephrectomy).
  • Chemotherapy and/or radiation therapy.

Prevention

  • Early genetic screening in high-risk families.
  • Regular pediatric checkups for early detection.
