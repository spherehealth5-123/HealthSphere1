Ataxia

Overview

Ataxia refers to a group of neurological disorders that affect balance, coordination, and speech. It results from damage to the cerebellum, spinal cord, or peripheral nerves, leading to difficulty in voluntary movements.

Causes

Ataxia can be caused by genetic mutations (hereditary ataxia), stroke, multiple sclerosis, tumors, chronic alcohol abuse, head trauma, or vitamin deficiencies.

Symptoms

  • Unsteady gait and poor balance
  • Difficulty with fine motor tasks
  • Slurred speech
  • Involuntary eye movements
  • Coordination problems in arms and legs

Treatment

There is no cure for most types of ataxia. Treatment focuses on physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, medications to manage symptoms, and addressing the underlying cause when possible.

Management

Supportive care, use of mobility aids, dietary supplements (like vitamin E or B12 if deficient), and genetic counseling may improve quality of life for people living with ataxia.
