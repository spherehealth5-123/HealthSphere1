Dysgraphia

Overview

Dysgraphia is a neurological learning disability that affects writing abilities, including handwriting and spelling.

Causes

Often linked to brain development issues, ADHD, or genetic factors.

Symptoms

  • Poor handwriting
  • Difficulty spelling
  • Struggling with written expression
  • Slow writing speed

Treatment

Occupational therapy, assistive technology, writing practice, and educational support.

Prevention

No known prevention; early intervention improves outcomes.
