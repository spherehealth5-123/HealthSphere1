Canavan Disease

Overview

Canavan disease is a rare inherited disorder that damages brain cells due to a deficiency of the enzyme aspartoacylase.

Causes

Caused by mutations in the ASPA gene, leading to abnormal buildup of N-acetylaspartic acid in the brain.

Symptoms

  • Developmental delays
  • Loss of motor skills
  • Poor muscle tone
  • Seizures

Treatment

No cure. Supportive treatments include physical therapy, seizure control, and nutritional support.

Prevention

Genetic counseling and carrier testing for at-risk families.
