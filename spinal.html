Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)

Overview

SMA is a genetic disorder affecting the motor nerve cells in the spinal cord, leading to muscle weakness and atrophy.

Causes

  • Mutation in the SMN1 gene
  • Inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern

Symptoms

  • Muscle weakness and poor motor control
  • Difficulty breathing and swallowing
  • Loss of reflexes

Treatment

Gene therapy, medications like nusinersen, physical therapy, and supportive breathing aids.

Prevention

No prevention; genetic counseling is recommended for families at risk.
