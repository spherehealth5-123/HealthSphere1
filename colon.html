Colon Cancer

Overview

Colon cancer develops in the large intestine (colon), usually beginning as benign polyps that can turn cancerous.

Causes

Caused by genetic mutations, poor diet, sedentary lifestyle, and family history of colorectal cancer.

Symptoms

  • Blood in stool
  • Persistent abdominal pain
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Changes in bowel habits

Treatment

Surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and radiation depending on cancer stage.

Prevention

Healthy diet, regular exercise, avoiding smoking, and colonoscopy screenings.
