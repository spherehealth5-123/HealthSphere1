Zellweger Syndrome

Overview

Zellweger syndrome is a rare genetic disorder affecting peroxisome biogenesis, leading to multiple organ dysfunction. Fatal in infancy.

Causes

  • Mutations in PEX genes affecting peroxisomes
  • Autosomal recessive inheritance

Symptoms

  • Hypotonia (weak muscles)
  • Seizures
  • Distinctive facial features
  • Liver dysfunction
  • Vision and hearing problems

Treatment

  • No cure; supportive treatment only
  • Manage seizures and liver issues
  • Physical therapy for muscle weakness

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for at-risk families
  • Prenatal testing if family history exists
