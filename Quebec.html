Quebec Platelet Disorder

Overview

Quebec Platelet Disorder (QPD) is a rare inherited bleeding disorder caused by abnormal platelet function leading to excessive bleeding.

Causes

  • Genetic mutation affecting fibrinolysis in platelets.
  • Family history of bleeding disorders.

Symptoms

  • Easy bruising and nosebleeds.
  • Heavy bleeding after surgery or injury.
  • Prolonged menstrual bleeding in women.

Treatment

No cure exists, but antifibrinolytic agents (like tranexamic acid) and proper surgical precautions help manage the condition.

Prevention

  • Avoid unnecessary surgeries or trauma.
  • Genetic counseling for affected families.
