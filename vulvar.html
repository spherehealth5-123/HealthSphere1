Vulvar Cancer

Overview

A rare type of cancer that occurs on the external female genitalia (vulva), often beginning as a lump or sore.

Causes

  • Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection.
  • Chronic vulvar irritation.
  • History of precancerous lesions.

Symptoms

  • Persistent itching or pain.
  • Lump or ulcer on the vulva.
  • Bleeding not related to menstruation.

Treatment

  • Surgery (removal of tumors).
  • Radiation therapy.
  • Chemotherapy in advanced cases.

Prevention

  • HPV vaccination.
  • Regular gynecological exams.
  • Early treatment of precancerous changes.
