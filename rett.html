Rett Syndrome

Overview

Rett Syndrome is a rare neurological disorder that affects brain development, leading to severe impairments in motor skills, communication, and growth, primarily in girls.

Causes

  • Mutations in the MECP2 gene
  • Genetic but not typically inherited

Symptoms

  • Loss of purposeful hand use
  • Slow head growth
  • Breathing problems and seizures

Treatment

No cure. Supportive care includes physical therapy, medications for seizures, and speech therapy.

Prevention

Cannot be prevented, but early diagnosis and therapy can improve quality of life.
