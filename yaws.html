Yaws

Overview

A tropical infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum pertenue, affecting skin, bones, and joints.

Causes

  • Direct skin-to-skin contact with an infected person.
  • Predominantly affects children in tropical regions.

Symptoms

  • Skin lesions, often in early stages.
  • Bone pain and swelling in later stages.
  • Joint pain and deformities if untreated.

Treatment

  • Single-dose antibiotics (penicillin or azithromycin).

Prevention

  • Avoid direct contact with infected individuals.
  • Mass treatment campaigns in endemic regions.
