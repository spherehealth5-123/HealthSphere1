Renal Cell Carcinoma

Overview

Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC) is the most common type of kidney cancer in adults. It develops in the lining of small tubes in the kidney.

Causes

  • Smoking and obesity
  • High blood pressure
  • Genetic conditions (e.g., Von Hippel-Lindau disease)

Symptoms

  • Blood in urine
  • Flank pain
  • Unexplained weight loss and fatigue

Treatment

Surgery (nephrectomy), targeted therapies, and immunotherapy.

Prevention

  • Avoid smoking
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Control blood pressure
