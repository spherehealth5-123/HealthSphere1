Cystic Fibrosis

Overview

Cystic fibrosis is a genetic disorder affecting the lungs and digestive system, leading to thick mucus buildup.

Causes

Mutations in the CFTR gene, inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern.

Symptoms

  • Chronic cough
  • Frequent lung infections
  • Poor growth
  • Digestive problems

Treatment

Airway clearance therapy, inhaled medicines, CFTR modulators, high-calorie diet.

Prevention

No prevention, but genetic counseling helps at-risk families.
