Zenker's Diverticulum

Overview

Zenker’s Diverticulum is a pouch that forms at the junction of the esophagus and throat, causing difficulty swallowing and regurgitation.

Causes

  • Weakness in the esophageal wall
  • Abnormal pressure during swallowing

Symptoms

  • Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing)
  • Regurgitation of undigested food
  • Chronic cough or bad breath
  • Throat discomfort

Treatment

  • Surgical removal of the diverticulum (diverticulectomy)
  • Endoscopic procedures for smaller pouches

Prevention

  • Early treatment of swallowing disorders
  • Avoid excessive pressure when swallowing
