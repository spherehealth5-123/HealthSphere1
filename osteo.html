Osteomyelitis

Overview

Osteomyelitis is an infection of the bone caused by bacteria or fungi, leading to inflammation and bone destruction if untreated.

Causes

It is most often caused by Staphylococcus aureus bacteria entering the bone through the bloodstream, surgery, or injury.

Symptoms

  • Severe bone pain
  • Swelling and redness over the affected area
  • Fever and chills
  • Fatigue
  • Restricted movement

Treatment

Treatment includes antibiotics, surgery to remove infected tissue, and pain management.

Prevention

Prevented by proper wound care, managing chronic conditions, and sterile surgical techniques.
