Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome

Overview

Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome is a rare disorder characterized by tumors (gastrinomas) in the pancreas or duodenum that cause excessive gastric acid production.

Causes

  • Gastrin-secreting tumors (gastrinomas)
  • Genetic mutations, sometimes linked to MEN1 syndrome

Symptoms

  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Diarrhea
  • Gastroesophageal reflux
  • Peptic ulcers

Treatment

  • Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) to reduce acid
  • Surgery to remove tumors
  • Chemotherapy if tumors are malignant

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling if family history of MEN1
  • Early screening for at-risk individuals
