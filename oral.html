Oral Cancer

Overview

Oral Cancer occurs in the mouth, tongue, lips, or throat and can be life-threatening if not detected early.

Causes

Main causes include tobacco use, alcohol consumption, HPV infection, and prolonged sun exposure (for lip cancer).

Symptoms

  • Sores in the mouth that do not heal
  • Pain in the mouth or throat
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • White or red patches in the mouth
  • Lump in the neck

Treatment

Treatment includes surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and targeted drugs.

Prevention

Prevented by avoiding tobacco, reducing alcohol, vaccination against HPV, and regular dental check-ups.
