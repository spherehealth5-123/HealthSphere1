Diffuse Large B-cell Lymphoma (DLBCL)

Overview

Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma is the most common type of non-Hodgkin lymphoma, a fast-growing blood cancer affecting B lymphocytes.

Causes

Caused by genetic mutations in B cells; risk factors include weakened immune system, infections, and family history.

Symptoms

  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Fever and night sweats
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue

Treatment

Chemotherapy (R-CHOP regimen), radiation therapy, targeted therapy, and stem cell transplant.

Prevention

No guaranteed prevention; maintaining a healthy immune system may reduce risk.
