Friedreich's Ataxia

Overview

Friedreich's ataxia is a rare inherited disease that causes progressive damage to the nervous system and movement problems.

Causes

Mutation in the FXN gene leading to reduced frataxin protein.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty walking
  • Loss of coordination
  • Heart disease
  • Scoliosis

Treatment

No cure; treatment includes physical therapy, medications for heart disease, and assistive devices.

Prevention

Genetic counseling for at-risk families.
