Thalassemia

Overview

Thalassemia is an inherited blood disorder where the body makes less hemoglobin than normal, leading to anemia.

Causes

  • Mutations in hemoglobin-producing genes
  • Inherited from parents

Symptoms

  • Severe anemia and fatigue
  • Bone deformities
  • Delayed growth

Treatment

Regular blood transfusions, iron chelation therapy, and bone marrow transplant.

Prevention

Genetic counseling and prenatal screening for carriers.
