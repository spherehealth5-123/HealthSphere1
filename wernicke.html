Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Overview

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome is a neurological disorder caused by thiamine (Vitamin B1) deficiency, often associated with chronic alcohol abuse.

Causes

  • Chronic alcoholism.
  • Severe malnutrition or prolonged vomiting.
  • Disorders affecting absorption of thiamine.

Symptoms

  • Confusion, memory loss, hallucinations.
  • Ataxia (loss of coordination).
  • Eye movement abnormalities (nystagmus, double vision).

Treatment

  • High-dose thiamine supplementation (oral or IV).
  • Abstinence from alcohol.
  • Supportive care and cognitive rehabilitation.

Prevention

  • Maintain a balanced diet with sufficient vitamin B1.
  • Limit alcohol consumption.
  • Early medical intervention for malnutrition or absorption issues.
