Zika Virus

Overview

Zika virus is a mosquito-borne flavivirus transmitted primarily by Aedes mosquitoes. It may cause mild symptoms in adults but severe birth defects in newborns.

Causes

  • Bite from infected Aedes mosquito
  • Sexual transmission from an infected partner
  • Mother-to-fetus transmission during pregnancy

Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Rash
  • Joint pain
  • Conjunctivitis (red eyes)
  • Muscle pain
  • Headache

Treatment

  • No specific antiviral treatment
  • Supportive care: rest, fluids, and acetaminophen
  • Avoid aspirin and NSAIDs until dengue is ruled out

Prevention

  • Use mosquito repellents
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing
  • Sleep under mosquito nets
  • Practice safe sex in outbreak areas
