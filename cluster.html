Cluster Headaches

Overview

Cluster headaches are severe, recurring headaches that occur in cyclical patterns or “clusters.”

Causes

Exact cause unknown, but linked to hypothalamus activity and abnormalities in blood vessels or nerves.

Symptoms

  • Severe one-sided head pain
  • Red or watery eyes
  • Restlessness
  • Swelling around the eye

Treatment

Oxygen therapy, triptan injections, preventive medications, and nerve stimulation therapy.

Prevention

Avoid alcohol, smoking, and known triggers. Preventive medicines can reduce attacks.
