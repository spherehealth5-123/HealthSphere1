Vocal Cord Dysfunction

Overview

A condition in which the vocal cords close instead of opening during breathing, causing shortness of breath and noisy breathing.

Causes

  • Exposure to irritants (smoke, chemicals).
  • Stress or anxiety.
  • Exercise-induced breathing problems.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty breathing, especially during exercise.
  • Hoarseness or voice changes.
  • Choking sensation in throat.

Treatment

  • Speech therapy for breathing techniques.
  • Relaxation and stress management.
  • Treating underlying conditions like acid reflux.

Prevention

  • Avoid respiratory irritants.
  • Practice controlled breathing exercises.
  • Manage stress effectively.
