Vaccinia

Overview

Vaccinia is a virus from the poxvirus family, historically used in the smallpox vaccine. It is related to but distinct from smallpox.

Causes

  • Caused by the vaccinia virus.
  • Spread through direct contact with lesions or contaminated materials.

Symptoms

  • Skin lesions or pustules
  • Fever and fatigue
  • Swollen lymph nodes

Treatment

  • Most cases resolve without treatment.
  • Severe infections may require antivirals like cidofovir.

Prevention

  • Proper vaccine administration techniques.
  • Avoid direct contact with open lesions.
