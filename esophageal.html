Esophageal Cancer

Overview

Esophageal cancer is a malignancy that develops in the lining of the esophagus, the tube that carries food from the throat to the stomach.

Causes

Smoking, heavy alcohol use, chronic acid reflux, obesity, and genetic factors.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Chest pain or heartburn
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Persistent cough or hoarseness

Treatment

Surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and immunotherapy.

Prevention

Avoid smoking and alcohol, maintain a healthy weight, and manage acid reflux.
