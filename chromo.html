Y-Chromosome Infertility

Overview

Y-Chromosome Infertility is a genetic condition where deletions or mutations in the Y chromosome impair sperm production in males.

Causes

  • Deletions or microdeletions in the Y chromosome.
  • Inherited genetic factors affecting sperm production.

Symptoms

  • Low sperm count or azoospermia (absence of sperm).
  • Difficulty conceiving naturally.

Treatment

  • Assisted reproductive technologies (IVF or ICSI).
  • Hormonal therapy if indicated.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for at-risk couples.
