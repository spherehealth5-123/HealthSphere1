X-Linked Agammaglobulinemia

Overview

X-Linked Agammaglobulinemia is a genetic disorder that impairs the body's ability to produce antibodies, making patients highly susceptible to infections.

Causes

  • Mutation in the BTK gene on the X chromosome.
  • Inherited in X-linked recessive pattern, mostly affecting males.

Symptoms

  • Recurrent bacterial infections.
  • Chronic diarrhea and respiratory infections.
  • Failure to thrive in infants.

Treatment

  • Regular immunoglobulin replacement therapy.
  • Prompt antibiotics for infections.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for families.
  • Early diagnosis and management to prevent severe infections.
