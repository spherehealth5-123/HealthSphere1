WAGR Syndrome

Overview

WAGR Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder characterized by Wilms tumor, Aniridia, Genitourinary anomalies, and intellectual disability.

Causes

  • Genetic deletion on chromosome 11.
  • Inherited or spontaneous mutation.

Symptoms

  • Absence of iris (aniridia).
  • Genitourinary malformations.
  • Wilms tumor in kidneys.
  • Developmental delays and intellectual disability.

Treatment

  • Regular kidney monitoring and tumor removal if needed.
  • Surgery for genitourinary anomalies.
  • Supportive therapy for developmental delays.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for families with history.
  • Early diagnosis through prenatal screening if risk exists.
