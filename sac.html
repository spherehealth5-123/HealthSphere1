Yolk Sac Tumor

Overview

A rare malignant tumor originating from germ cells, often occurring in children, mainly affecting the ovaries or testes.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations during germ cell development.
  • Mostly sporadic, exact cause often unknown.

Symptoms

  • Abdominal mass or swelling.
  • Pain in affected area.
  • Fever, nausea, or unexplained weight loss in some cases.

Treatment

  • Surgical removal of tumor.
  • Chemotherapy for malignant tumors.

Prevention

  • No specific prevention known; early detection improves outcomes.
