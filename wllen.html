Wallenberg's Syndrome

Overview

Wallenberg's Syndrome, also called lateral medullary syndrome, is a neurological condition caused by a stroke in the lateral part of the medulla oblongata.

Causes

  • Blockage or clot in the vertebral or posterior inferior cerebellar artery.
  • Stroke due to high blood pressure or atherosclerosis.

Symptoms

  • Vertigo, nausea, and vomiting.
  • Difficulty swallowing and speaking.
  • Loss of pain and temperature sensation on one side of the body.
  • Horner's syndrome (drooping eyelid, constricted pupil).

Treatment

  • Stroke management: anticoagulants or thrombolytics.
  • Physical and speech therapy.
  • Supportive care for swallowing and balance issues.

Prevention

  • Control risk factors for stroke: hypertension, diabetes, and smoking.
  • Regular cardiovascular checkups.
