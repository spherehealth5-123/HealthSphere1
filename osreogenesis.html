Osteogenesis Imperfecta

Overview

Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI) is a rare genetic disorder also known as brittle bone disease, causing fragile bones that break easily.

Causes

Caused by mutations in the COL1A1 or COL1A2 genes affecting collagen production.

Symptoms

  • Frequent bone fractures
  • Short stature
  • Blue sclera (eye whites)
  • Hearing loss
  • Loose joints and weak muscles

Treatment

No cure, but treatment includes bisphosphonate therapy, physical therapy, mobility aids, and surgery.

Prevention

Cannot be prevented; genetic counseling may help families at risk.
