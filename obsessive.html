Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Overview

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder is a mental health condition characterized by persistent unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

Causes

Causes include genetic predisposition, brain chemistry imbalances, and environmental stressors.

Symptoms

  • Repetitive checking or cleaning
  • Unwanted intrusive thoughts
  • Excessive organization
  • Anxiety and distress
  • Compulsive rituals

Treatment

Treatment involves cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), medications (SSRIs), and stress management.

Prevention

No guaranteed prevention, but early intervention, therapy, and stress reduction can help manage symptoms.
