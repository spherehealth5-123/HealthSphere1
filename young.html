Young-Onset Parkinson's Disease

Overview

A neurological disorder affecting movement, occurring in individuals younger than 50, characterized by tremors, stiffness, and slow movement.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations (PARK2, PARK7, PINK1, etc.).
  • Environmental factors such as toxins.

Symptoms

  • Tremors, especially in hands.
  • Slowness of movement (bradykinesia).
  • Muscle rigidity.
  • Balance and coordination problems.

Treatment

  • Medications such as Levodopa and dopamine agonists.
  • Physical therapy and occupational therapy.
  • Surgical options (Deep Brain Stimulation) in severe cases.

Prevention

  • No guaranteed prevention, but healthy lifestyle and avoiding toxins may reduce risk.
